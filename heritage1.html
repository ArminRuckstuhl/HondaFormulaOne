Our First Steps into Formula One

Throughout his life, Honda's founder, Soichiro Honda, had an interest in automobiles. Initially starting Honda to produce piston rings for Toyota, Honda eventually pivoted to making inprovised motorized bicycles that utilized left over generator engines as power units. After beginning to manufacter motorcycle engines after the supply of generator engines dried up, Honda soon began to enter motorcycle races around Japan to resounding success.

In 1959, Honda entered the famed Isle of Man TT motorcycle race for the first time. Known for its incredibly technical and high speed track, the Isle of Man TT is the most brutal and one of the most well known MotoGP races. Honda entered in four 125cc bikes. This first challenge resulted in Honda claiming 6th, 7th, 8th and 11th in the 125cc light weight class, as well as the Manufacturers' Team Award. Soon after, Soichiro Honda began looking to other forms of motorsport to flex his high speed muscles and began eyeing Formula One as a possible option. This then led to the creation of one of the most revolutionary Formula One Teams today.
