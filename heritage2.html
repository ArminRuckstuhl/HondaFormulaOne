Honda’s First Years in Formula One

Beginning in 1964, Honda began racing in Formula One under the Honda Formula One Name. They joined Ferrari and BRM and developed and constructed the engine and the chassis of their first car, the RA271. Their first year first year of competing led to decent results, coming fifth in one race and fourth in another with American drivers Ronnie Bucknum and Richie Ginther piloting the two Honda vehicles.

After some development over the winter break, Honda debuted their new car, the RA272. This car proved to be more successful than their first car, mostly due to the new rules that required engines to be 3.0 litre V12 engines. They also managed to finally take the top step on the podium this year, after the Ginther won the Mexican Grand Prix that year. Honda returned back to the winners circle in 1967 when the new RA300 won the first race of the season, the Italian Grand Prix, with John Surtees piloting the machine. That year, Honda finished fourth int he constructors championship, their highest placement so far.

The 1968 season however, was not the best season for Honda, plagued with reliability problems from their new car, the RA301, they still managed to pull of two podiums and a pole position. However, a crash in the French Grand Prix that resulted in the death of one of their drivers, Jo Schlesser. This death and the new focus of wanting to transform the company into an affordible town car company led Honda to pull out of Formula One after the season.
