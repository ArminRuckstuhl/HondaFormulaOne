Honda as an Engine Supplier

Honda reentered Formula One as an engine supplier in 1983 originally for the Sprint team. They partnered with various other teams over the next decade including Tyrrell, Williams, and Lotus although their most lucrative partnership came with the McLaren Racing Team. In 1988, Honda developed the RA168E engine which went into the Mclaren MP4/4 which was piloted by Alain Prost and Ayrton Senna. Alain Prost had been racing for McLaren Racing since 1984 and was already a three time World Champion. Ayrton Senna was joining from Tyrell where the previous year, he had been in contention for the Championship.

The MP4/4 proved to be one fo the best cars Formula One had ever seen, clocking in at just over 700bhp and able to rev up to 13,000rpm, it was also one of the most powerful. Combined with Ayrton Senna's clinical and incredibly precise driving, it was a recipe for success. In its first outing at the Brazillian Grand Prix, the two McLaren cars qualified first and second, being divided last year's World Champion, Nigel Mansel. McLaren went on to dominate the 1988 season by winning 15 out of the 16 races that they competed in.

Another amazing story of the MP4/4 that truly revealed just how dominant it was occured at the San Marino Grand Prix where Senna and Prost qualified first and second with times in the 1:27s. No other car, not even cars that had the same engine as the McLaren, could even qualify anywhere close to the two McLarens. Third on the grid was World Champion Nelson Piquet who qualified with a time of 1:30.500, a full three seconds shy of the two McLarens.
